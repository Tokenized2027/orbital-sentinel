# Video Visual Brief v2: Mission Control

Cinematic 2 minute 40 second tech explainer video. Dark mode aesthetic. Mission control / war room feel. Professional tone. Clean typography. Amber and cyan accent palette on deep black. Subtle data-driven motion graphics. Feels operational, not fantasy.

## Scene 1: The Problem (0:00 - 0:30)

Open on a dark screen. A single blinking cursor. Then a live price chart appears, heartbeat style, filling the frame. Steady rhythm. Clean. Calm.

Then the line stutters. A second chart appears next to it. A third. A fourth. More and more data streams tiling across the screen like security camera feeds. Pool balances. Bridge transfers. Vault utilization rates. Token movements. All running simultaneously.

One feed flashes red. Then another. The charts start drifting, losing sync. Numbers spike. A balance drops to zero. The feeds keep multiplying but nobody responds. The red alerts pile up unanswered.

Camera pulls back slowly to reveal: all these feeds are playing on a wall of monitors in an empty room. No one is watching.

On-screen text, clean sans-serif, appears one line at a time:

"DeFi manages billions."
"The data is all there."
"Nobody's watching."

Hard cut to black.

## Scene 2: What It Does (0:30 - 1:20)

Fade in. Same monitor wall. But now there's a presence. A clean interface boots up across all screens simultaneously. The Sentinel logo resolves in the center.

One by one, eight monitoring panels tile across the wall. Each panel has a clean header label and live-updating data beneath it. As each one activates, a subtle pulse of light connects it to the center.

Treasury. Price Feeds. Governance. Vaults.
Whale Flows. Bridge Status. Curve Pools. LINK AI Arbitrage.

The camera moves laterally along the monitor wall, showing each panel's data refreshing. Not abstract glowing orbs. Actual numbers. Actual risk levels. Clean data visualization: bar charts, percentage fills, status indicators.

As each panel processes its data, a small lock icon appears in its corner. The lock represents the proof hash being written. When it clicks shut, a transaction hash briefly appears beneath it, then fades.

On-screen text overlaid:

"8 CRE workflows."
"Live Ethereum data."
"Every assessment, proven on-chain."

Camera settles on the LINK AI Arbitrage panel. It's slightly larger than the others. A GPT analysis result renders line by line inside it: premium basis points, confidence score, execution signal. The lock clicks shut.

Small label beneath: "Live on Chainlink DON. 7x/day. Autonomous."

## Scene 3: How It Connects (1:20 - 2:10)

The camera angle shifts. We're now looking at the monitor wall from above, like a mission control overview.

Thin lines of light begin connecting the eight panels. Data flows along the lines toward a ninth panel in the center: "Composite Intelligence."

The composite panel shows a condensed view: key metrics from five workflows feeding into a single analysis. The AI processes them. A recommendation renders. A golden lock icon appears, larger than the others. It clicks shut with weight.

Cut to a clean, stylized blockchain explorer view. Not Etherscan exactly, but inspired by it. Dark background. Clean rows. Each row is a HealthRecorded event with a workflow prefix tag: treasury:ok, feeds:ok, laa:ok. Each row appears with a brief amber glow, then settles into the list.

The list scrolls. Row after row. Proof after proof.

On-screen counter in the corner, ticking up the proof count from SentinelRegistry

Camera pulls back to show the full explorer view alongside the dashboard. Two sides of the same system: the intelligence and the proof.

## Scene 4: Wrap (2:10 - 2:40)

Return to the monitor wall, now fully operational. All eight panels active. Data flowing. Locks clicking at steady intervals. The room feels alive, purposeful.

The camera slowly pulls back through a doorway, as if leaving the control room. The monitors shrink into the background but remain visible, still running, still watching.

Final text sequence, centered on black, each line appearing with a quiet beat:

"Autonomous."
"Verifiable."
"On-chain."

Brief pause.

"Sentinel by Orbital."

Final line:

"DeFi shouldn't fly blind."

Slow fade to black. Silence.

---

## Production Notes

**Color palette:** Deep black (#0d1117), amber (#f0883e), cyan (#58a6ff), white text. No bright neon. Muted, professional.

**Motion style:** Deliberate, not frantic. Slow lateral camera moves. Data appears at a readable pace. Locks click with satisfying weight. No swooping 3D orbits.

**Typography:** Mono for data/hashes, clean sans-serif for statements. Large, high contrast, minimal words per frame.

**Sound design:** Low ambient hum. Subtle data processing clicks. Lock mechanisms. No dramatic music drops. Quiet confidence.

**Key difference from v1:** v1 is a sci-fi concept film (floating cities, holographic orbs). v2 is a war room documentary. Everything on screen could plausibly be a real monitoring system. The drama comes from the data itself, not from abstract visuals.
